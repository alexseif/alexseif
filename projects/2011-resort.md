# Project Dossier
Retained to architect a high-end property rental and resort management system capable of competing with early global booking engines. The platform reconciled conflicting operational demands: open public guest bookings, direct private owner occupancy, strict contractual legal constraints, and complex real-time operational turnover tracking.

### Core Architecture & System Engineering
- **Relational Yield & Pricing Architecture:** Engineered a resilient billing and reservation matrix that calculated highly fluid variables in real-time. Shifted complex pricing equations into highly optimized database Views, loading instant matrices on the UI to protect frontend execution threads.
- **Contractual State Logic:** Codified physical property management contracts into the core business logic. The engine automatically enforced variable booking windows and cancellation policies unique to each owner, fundamentally blocking inventory conflict before transactional execution.

### Engineering Notes / Edge Cases
Demonstrates trench pedigree through the independent engineering and management of the entire infrastructure stack. Provisioned secure staging environments, implemented automated deployments, and authored rigorous disaster recovery (DR) protocols to ensure multi-tenant operational continuity.

# Use Case Case Study: Multi-Tenant Yield Engines
### 1. Objective & Scope
Build a resilient operational framework to replace fragmented manual property tracking with a centralized, conflict-free state engine.

### 2. Technical Decisions & Implementation
Programmatically integrated real-time resort maintenance workflows into the availability calendar. Enforced a mandatory, dual-state operational lock before and after every booking, auto-generating work orders for physical property inspections and multi-owner hardware billing splits. The implementation bypassed fragile plugin ecosystems for strict, bespoke database state-preservation matrices.
